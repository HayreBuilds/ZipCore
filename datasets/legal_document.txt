FEDERAL DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC - DIGITAL SERVICES ACT
Act No. 1234/2026

PREAMBLE

WHEREAS the Government of the Federal Democratic Republic recognizes the fundamental importance of digital transformation in public service delivery;

WHEREAS the protection of citizen data and privacy constitutes a paramount obligation of the State;

WHEREAS efficient data management systems are essential for the proper functioning of government institutions;

NOW, THEREFORE, in accordance with the Constitution, the following Act is hereby enacted:

CHAPTER ONE: GENERAL PROVISIONS

Article 1 - Short Title
This Act may be cited as the "Digital Services Act No. 1234/2026."

Article 2 - Definitions
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires:
(a) "Digital Service" means any service provided through electronic means by government institutions to citizens, businesses, or other entities;
(b) "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person;
(c) "Data Controller" means the government body which determines the purposes and means of processing personal data;
(d) "Data Processor" means any entity which processes personal data on behalf of the Data Controller;
(e) "Consent" means any freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of the data subject's wishes;
(f) "Processing" means any operation performed on personal data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, dissemination, alignment, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

Article 3 - Scope of Application
(1) This Act shall apply to all government institutions that collect, process, store, or transmit citizen data through digital platforms.
(2) This Act shall extend to private entities contracted by government institutions for data processing purposes.
(3) This Act shall not apply to data processing activities conducted solely for national security purposes, subject to oversight by the designated authority.

Article 4 - Principles of Data Processing
All data processing under this Act shall adhere to the following principles:
(a) Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency;
(b) Purpose limitation;
(c) Data minimization;
(d) Accuracy;
(e) Storage limitation;
(f) Integrity and confidentiality;
(g) Accountability.

CHAPTER TWO: DATA COMPRESSION AND STORAGE

Article 5 - Mandatory Data Optimization
(1) All government digital platforms shall implement approved data compression mechanisms to optimize storage utilization.
(2) Only lossless compression algorithms shall be employed for citizen records, legal documents, and any data where integrity is paramount.
(3) The approved compression methods include, but are not limited to: Huffman Coding, Lempel-Ziv-Welch (LZW), and Arithmetic Coding.

Article 6 - Compression Standards
(1) Compressed data must achieve a minimum compression ratio of 1.5:1 for standard text documents.
(2) All compressed data must be accompanied by metadata sufficient for decompression.
(3) Regular integrity checks shall be performed to ensure that decompressed data exactly matches the original.

Article 7 - Data Transmission
(1) All inter-agency data transmission shall utilize compressed formats to minimize bandwidth consumption.
(2) Transmission protocols must include error detection and correction mechanisms.
(3) Data shall be encrypted prior to compression when transmitted over public networks.

CHAPTER THREE: PENALTIES AND ENFORCEMENT

Article 8 - Violations
Any person or entity that:
(a) Employs lossy compression on protected data categories shall be liable to a fine not exceeding 500,000 Birr;
(b) Fails to maintain decompression capability for archived data shall be liable to a fine not exceeding 300,000 Birr;
(c) Transmits uncompressed data in violation of Article 7 shall receive a written warning for the first offense and a fine not exceeding 100,000 Birr for subsequent offenses.

Article 9 - Effective Date
This Act shall come into force on the date of its publication in the Federal Gazette.

ENACTED by the House of Peoples' Representatives on this 1st day of January, 2026.
